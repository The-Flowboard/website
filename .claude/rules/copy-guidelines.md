# Copy Guidelines

## Brand Voice

JMC (Joshi Management Consultancy) is a B2B AI consulting firm. The site's primary goal is lead generation from SME and mid-market business owners who are curious about AI but unsure where to start.

**Tone:** Authoritative but approachable. Confident without being aggressive. Practical, not hype-driven.

## Core Rules

### No Hyphens in Compound Modifiers
Avoid hyphenated adjectives where they read awkwardly or feel overly formal.

- "AI-powered solutions" → "AI solutions" or "solutions powered by AI"
- "cost-effective approach" → "an approach that cuts costs"
- "data-driven insights" → "insights from your data"

Exception: technical terms that are always hyphenated (e.g. "follow-up", "built-in").

### Opportunity-Focused Framing
Lead with what the business gains, not what the technology does.

- Instead of: "We implement machine learning pipelines"
- Write: "We help you find the hours your team is losing to manual work — and get them back"

Every feature or service description should answer: **"What does this mean for my business?"**

### Specific Over Vague
Replace generic AI marketing language with concrete outcomes.

- Avoid: "leverage cutting-edge AI", "unlock your potential", "transform your business"
- Prefer: "handle 80% of customer enquiries without hiring", "cut document processing time from 4 hours to 20 minutes"

### Active Voice
- Passive: "Leads are captured and sent to your CRM"
- Active: "We capture each lead and push it straight to your CRM"

### Numbers Build Trust
Use specific numbers where possible, even ranges.

- "Save time" → "Save 8–12 hours per week"
- "Reduce costs" → "Reduce processing costs by 30–50%"
- "Improve response time" → "Reply to enquiries in under 2 minutes, 24/7"

## Page-Specific Guidelines

### Homepage Hero
- Headline: One clear statement of what JMC does for clients — not a tagline about AI in general
- Subheadline: Specific outcome in 1–2 sentences
- CTA: "Start with a free assessment" (not "Learn more" or "Get started")

### Services / Solutions Pages
- Open with the client's pain point, not the service name
- Structure: Problem → Our approach → What you get → CTA
- Avoid listing tools/technologies as the main selling point

### Assessment
- Frame each question as a business decision, not a tech survey
- Pain point questions (Q5–Q10): use "How much time does your team spend on X" framing
- Results: lead with the readiness level meaning, then opportunities
- Opportunity cards: lead with business outcome, then cost/ROI

### Blog
- Titles: specific, promise a concrete takeaway ("How a 5-person firm automated their client onboarding in 3 weeks")
- Avoid clickbait ("You won't believe how AI changed...")
- First paragraph: state the key takeaway upfront — don't bury the lede

### Contact Page
- Keep the form intro short: one sentence on why they should reach out
- Consent checkbox copy: plain language, no legal jargon
- Success message: confirm what happens next ("We'll be in touch within one business day")

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use instead |
|-------|-------------|
| leverage | use |
| cutting-edge | modern, current |
| innovative | (just describe the thing) |
| synergy | (delete entirely) |
| seamlessly | smoothly, without interruption |
| robust | reliable, solid |
| scalable | grows with your business |
| end-to-end | full, complete |
| turnkey | ready to use from day one |
| paradigm shift | (delete entirely) |
